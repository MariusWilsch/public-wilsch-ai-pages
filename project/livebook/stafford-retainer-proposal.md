# EasyBooks — Retainer Proposal

[[client-stafford]]

---

## Why This Proposal Exists

EasyBooks has a clear vision — four engines (Vulture, Plug, Opera, Shakespeare) turning lived experience into lasting books. The ambition is real.

The specifications are not yet aligned with the ambition. Five documented contradictions exist between your technical documents and our conversations — including Vulture's position as both priority one and build position five, and a Plug dependency that blocks the engine you want built first.

These contradictions are not criticism — they are evidence that the vision is evolving faster than the specs. Any developer building against unresolved specs builds the wrong thing. Resolving them is where the value starts.

This proposal covers that resolution — and everything that follows from it.

## What You Get

Your vision turned into buildable requirements — across all four engines. Not a developer guessing at specs. An AI architect who learns your business, resolves the contradictions, and produces specifications that any developer can build from.

**The retainer produces:**

| Bi-weekly sprint | What gets delivered |
|------------------|--------------------|
| **Sprint 1** | Design sprint with Stafford — resolve Vulture's input/output triple, define 3 niches, generate sample content. Vulture V1 spec. |
| **Sprint 2** | Developer builds from spec through the system. Bi-weekly demo — Stafford sees progress, adjusts priorities. |
| **Sprint 3+** | Iterate — what worked, what didn't, adjust scope. System gets smarter every sprint. |

**The developer is pluggable.** Whoever builds — my developer or yours — runs through the same system. The system makes any developer productive. Who builds is a separate conversation. What matters: the specs are right before anyone writes a line of code.

## Terms

**Duration:** 3 months. Long enough to prove value, short enough that neither side is trapped.

**Cadence:** Bi-weekly sprints. Each sprint: agree on deliverables → build → demo. Progress visible every two weeks.

**3-month review:** At the end of month 3, both sides evaluate:
- What was delivered
- What commercial outcomes resulted
- Whether to continue, adjust scope, or end cleanly
- Success fee structure (defined from actual results, not projections)

**Non-exclusive:** I serve multiple clients concurrently.

**Exit:** Either side can end at the 3-month mark. No lock-in beyond that. If it's not working, we part cleanly. You keep everything produced.

**Your obligations (Mitbringpflichten):** The retainer requires your active participation. Specifically:
- Attend the bi-weekly design sprint / demo (2-3 hours per sprint)
- Make scope decisions when presented with options — unresolved decisions block the sprint
- Provide access to documents, data, and stakeholders as needed
- Respond to async questions within 48 hours
- Be available for extraction sessions (30-60 min) when requirements need clarification — your domain knowledge is the input

If milestones are blocked because these obligations aren't met, the retainer applies regardless.
