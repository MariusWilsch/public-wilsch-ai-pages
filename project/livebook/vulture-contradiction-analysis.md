# Vulture — Contradiction Analysis
[[client-stafford]]

Five contradictions between Stafford's verbal priorities, his technical documents, and agreed scope from previous calls. Evidence for why a design sprint is required before any build commitment.

---

## 1. Priority vs Build Order

**Stafford's priority (Mar 14):**
> "That's priority one." — on Vulture

**His own March Technical Plan, Section 11 — Build Order:**
> "5. Vulture automation (only once governance is stable)"

Vulture is priority one in conversation. Position five of six in his own technical specification.

---

## 2. Vulture First vs Plug Dependency

**Stafford's ask (Mar 14):**
> "I need Vulture completed before then" [before the app rebuild]

**His own March Technical Plan — Plug Analytics section:**
> "Without this: Vulture learns incorrectly." — on Plug feeding performance data back to Vulture

His system flow: `Vulture → Plug → Opera → Shakespeare`. Vulture feeds INTO Plug, but needs Plug's data to LEARN. He asks for the upstream before the downstream exists.

---

## 3. Scope Reversal Without New Information

**Mar 10 call:**
Vulture explicitly excluded from V1. Stafford did not push back. Marius: "I was personally thinking that Vulture should also not be part of V1." Stafford's response: pivoted to starting channels ASAP, mentioned getting someone else to build it.

**Mar 14 call (4 days later):**
> "That's priority one."

Complete reversal. No new documents, no new technical input, no new market signal between calls — only increased urgency.

---

## 4. Definition Gap

**His EB Business Master Plan:**
Self-evolving funnels, A/B testing, cultural signal detection, behaviour-based funnel extension, AI lifecycle growth engine, social content repurposing engine.

**His Mar 14 transcript:**
> "The main part of Vulture is actually producing me content. Marketing content. Developing content."

Two different systems described by the same person. The documents describe a sophisticated growth automation engine. The conversation describes content production.

---

## 5. Ownership Confusion

**Mar 10 — Separation model:**
> "It may better for me just to get that part built. And you just go, right, I'm gonna deal with the agents." — Stafford suggesting he gets Vulture built separately

**Mar 14 — Marius builds it:**
> "So if you come back to me, let me know how long it's gonna take." — asking Marius for a Vulture timeline

**Mar 14 — Hire also builds AI:**
> "I want them [hire] to build the background AI as well as work with Vulture in managing what it's doing."

Three different ownership models across two calls: (A) Stafford gets Vulture built by someone else, (B) Marius builds Vulture, (C) Stafford's hire builds AI + manages Vulture.
