# Omega's Pearl POC Results
[[client-omega]]

Proof of concept: AI pearl detection from Omega's Learn to Sing class recordings. 3 videos transcribed, 6 pearls extracted across all 4 taxonomy categories.

**Pipeline:** `yt-dlp` → `assemblyai` transcription → Claude pearl detection against [design doc taxonomy](https://mariuswilsch.github.io/public-wilsch-ai-pages/project/WILSCH-AI-INTERNAL/omega-clipping-service-design)

## Pearl 1 — Quotable Wisdom

**Video:** [Learn 2 Sing with Carlos](https://youtube.com/watch?v=dfw6GavSAKU&t=506s) (34 min)
**Timestamp:** 8:26

> "A baby can hoop and holler all day and not get hoarse or tired. Because they're screaming on their breath."

**Context:** Omega teaches Carlos about breath support — the difference between speaking "down here on your voice" versus singing on the breath. She uses the baby analogy to explain why breath-supported singing is sustainable.

**Why this is a pearl:** Standalone insight that reframes something everyone observes (babies screaming) into a vocal technique principle. Works as a text overlay clip or Pinterest image. Immediately quotable without needing context.

## Pearl 2 — Coaching Feedback

**Video:** [Learn 2 Sing with Carlos](https://youtube.com/watch?v=dfw6GavSAKU&t=942s) (34 min)
**Timestamp:** 15:42 – 16:43

> "You are not Alejandro Fernandez. You have to sing it from Carlos's point of view. Who are you singing to? ... I need you to sing into that person's soul. ... Your job as a singer is to give permission — so that everyone can feel the same way."

**Context:** Carlos chose a Spanish love song. Omega pushes him past imitation into personal expression — who is he singing to? A real person. The coaching builds from technique (choose your person) to philosophy (the singer's job is permission-giving). Carlos visibly shifts from performing to connecting.

**Why this is a pearl:** Demonstrates the before→coaching→after arc. Carlos starts uncertain, receives deep personalized coaching, and by the end Omega celebrates: "here you are on TV for the whole world to see." Shows Omega's method in action.

## Pearl 3 — Authenticity Breakthrough

**Video:** [Learn to Sing with Tina](https://youtube.com/watch?v=J7EN-wrqWig&t=779s) (42 min)
**Timestamp:** 12:59 – 13:29

> "Classical teachers will hate that. But they want everyone to sound the same, as opposed to Bettina being Bettina, Omega being Omega. ... I'm more interested in you being your unique sound because, like, in AI, people are going to get tired of the AI Voice. And we're going to be able to tell that that's a real person singing versus an AI voice. And so we want to hear your accent coming through. Shining through."

**Context:** Bettina is a German speaker learning to sing in English. Instead of correcting her accent, Omega reframes it as her superpower — the thing that makes her irreplaceable, especially in an AI world. This is Omega's core philosophy: "I'm not looking for another Whitney Houston. I'm looking for the next natural singer."

**Why this is a pearl:** Captures Omega's teaching philosophy in one moment. The AI-vs-human angle is timely and attention-grabbing. Shows the target avatar what they get: permission to be themselves, not a copy of someone else.

## Pearl 4 — Coaching Feedback

**Video:** [Learn to Sing with Tina](https://youtube.com/watch?v=J7EN-wrqWig&t=1609s) (42 min)
**Timestamp:** 26:49 – 27:20

> "The song 'I Will Always Love You' — who do you know sang that? ... Whitney Houston. But she didn't write the song. Dolly Parton, one of the greatest songwriters of all time, wrote that song. ... And this song could be Bettina's song. Not Rod Stewart. Not Clearwater. It could be your song because you're gonna craft it so that it fits your voice. Because the songwriter is just a vessel. And you don't put on someone else's pants and then become that person."

**Context:** Bettina is unsure how to make "Have You Ever Seen the Rain" her own. Omega uses the Whitney Houston / Dolly Parton comparison to show that the most famous version of a song is never the original — it's the one that fits the singer's voice. This gives Bettina permission to change phrasing, key, and emphasis.

**Why this is a pearl:** Universal insight wrapped in a famous example. The "songwriter is just a vessel" line is quotable on its own. Demonstrates Omega's method of teaching through storytelling, not instruction.

## Pearl 5 — Transformation Arc

**Video:** [Learn 2 Sing with Ira](https://youtube.com/watch?v=6kfUCAEKe7U&t=1628s) (81 min)
**Timestamp:** 27:08 – 29:09

> **Ira:** "I had a dark chapter in my life where I was using a lot of drugs. For me this song is about leaving this drug addict personality. It's bittersweet — the bitter about the past but also the sweet because this clarity and stillness happens."
>
> **Omega:** "I love that because you telling how good life can be after — that's going to help someone else find their acceptance with their Major Tom moments."

**Context:** Ira chose David Bowie's "Space Oddity" for her concert programme. When Omega asks what the song means to her, Ira reveals her recovery story — Major Tom floating away = the dissociation of addiction, Ground Control = reality calling her back. Omega immediately connects this personal story to the concert's impact on the audience.

**Why this is a pearl:** The most emotionally powerful moment across all 3 videos. Shows a real transformation arc — not just vocal technique, but life story woven into musical performance. This is what Omega means by "craft the song so it fits your story." The audience sees themselves in Ira's vulnerability.

## Pearl 6 — Coaching Feedback

**Video:** [Learn 2 Sing with Ira](https://youtube.com/watch?v=6kfUCAEKe7U&t=2753s) (81 min)
**Timestamp:** 45:53 – 47:55

> "I want you to handwrite it. Because you're still reading. You need to put the lyrics inside your body."
>
> "The difference between Major Tom and Ground Control has to be stark. Major Tom needs to be slippery. Ground Control needs to be more marchy."

**Context:** Ira is singing from a screen, still reading lyrics instead of performing. Omega's instruction to handwrite = physicalize the song, move it from the screen into muscle memory. Then she defines two vocal characters: slippery Major Tom (floating in space) vs marchy Ground Control (grounded authority). This gives Ira a clear performance framework.

**Why this is a pearl:** Concrete, actionable coaching that transforms an early read-through into a dramatic one. "Put the lyrics inside your body" is standalone quotable. The Major Tom / Ground Control contrast is the kind of specific, domain-expert coaching that generic clipping tools cannot detect.
